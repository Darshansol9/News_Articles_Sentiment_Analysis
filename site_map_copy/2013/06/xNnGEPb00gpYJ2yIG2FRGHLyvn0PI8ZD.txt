Story highlights Fisherman describes a 6-foot wave near the New Jersey coastTidal gauges also detect tsunami-like wavesScientists are studying what caused the waves, the National Weather Service saysScientists are studying what caused tsunami-like waves that observers spotted near the New Jersey coast earlier this month."The source is complex and still under review," the National Weather Service's West Coast/Alaska Tsunami Warning Center says on its websiteIn New Jersey's Barnegat Inlet, a fisherman said he saw a massive, 6-foot-tall wave surging toward the coast on June 13. Tsunami-like waves were observed at more than 30 tide gauges in areas along the East Coast, the center said.Meteorological factors and the slumping of the continental shelf east of New Jersey may have played a role, the center said.
