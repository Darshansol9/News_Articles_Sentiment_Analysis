By Harmeet Shah Singh and Tom Watkins, CNNPartna, India (CNN) -- A week after an Indian school served toxic food to students, leaving 23 dead, its headmistress remains missing along with her husband, police said Tuesday. A nine-member team of officers has been formed to investigate and track down the principal, Meena Kumari, police superintendent Sujeet Kumar said.Police presence is heavy in the village in Bihar state, especially around the principal's home.Authorities have recorded statements from 40 witnesses, including child survivors of the July 16 food poisoning, Kumar said.Residents went on a rampage a day after the toxic meals were served in the local government school, torching at least four police cars.Read More
