By Laura Smith-Spark, CNN(CNN) -- Pakistani schoolgirl Malala Yousafzai has said 40 girls in Pakistan will be the first to benefit from a fund set up in her name after she was shot in the head by the Taliban for her efforts to promote girls' education.She announced the $45,000 grant for education in the Swat Valley -- the Taliban stronghold where she's from -- in a video played at the Women in the World summit in New York City on Thursday."We are going to educate 40 girls, and I invite all of you to support the Malala Fund," she said."Let us turn the education of 40 girls into 40 million girls."Read More
