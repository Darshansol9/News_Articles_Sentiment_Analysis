By Emily Jane Fox, CNNMoneyNew York (CNNMoney) -- Susan Patton isn't about "leaning in" or "leaning back." She seems to be leading the discussion about women in a whole different direction.In a letter titled "Advice for the young women of Princeton" published in the Daily Princetonian last week, the Ivy League alum said the path to happiness lies in their ability to land a husband during their four years at school."Find a husband on campus before you graduate," she wrote. The letter went viral, causing the college newspaper's site to crash.Patton said that men have a broader time frame in which to build a home and a family.
