By The CNN Wire(CNN) -- The Obama administration said Thursday it immediately will begin reviewing all new immigration cases to make sure they comply with administration priorities -- which focus on pursuing cases involving criminals who endanger public safety or national security, while dropping cases against immigrants with no criminal histories.Beginning December 4, officials will begin to apply the same criteria to the backlog of some 300,000 deportation cases pending in federal immigration courts, beginning with courts in Baltimore and Denver.The policy was touted by administration officials as "smart and effective immigration enforcement."Immigrant communities have long sought the change, but critics blast it as a "backdoor amnesty program."Read More
