Story highlights Firing squads were an option in Utah before they were banned in 2004New law would allow them as execution method if proper drugs not available for lethal injectionMany states are having problems getting the drugs(CNN) A bill that would allow Utah to use firing squads to execute some death-row prisoners passed in the state Senate on Tuesday.The measure, which passed 18 to 10, will be sent to Gov. Gary Herbert, who has not said whether he will sign it into law or veto the bill.Herbert released a statement on the legislation, which would add death by firing squad as an option in a state that uses lethal injection."Our statute is clear that lethal injection is the method by which (an execution) will happen. We have no intent to change that," he said.The law would give the state the option to use a five-member squad in cases where the drugs necessary for lethal injection aren't available 30 days before the date set for the execution.Read More
