Story highlights Ana is the first named storm of the 2015 Atlantic tropical storm seasonTropical storm alerts are in effect for some areas of the Carolinas(CNN) The first Atlantic tropical storm of the year appears poised to hit the shores of North Carolina or South Carolina by early Sunday.The center of Tropical Storm Ana spun in the Atlantic Ocean on Saturday afternoon, about 85 miles southeast of Myrtle Beach, South Carolina, as of 2 p.m. ET.Ana, with maximum sustained winds of 60 mph Saturday afternoon, is expected to hit land near the states' border by Sunday morning, the National Weather Service said.It may weaken as it crosses cooler water near the coast, but it should pack noticeable winds and rain and storm surges.Ana generally could drop 1 to 3 inches of rain, with isolated amounts of up to 5 inches, over the eastern Carolinas through Monday, according to the weather service.Read More
