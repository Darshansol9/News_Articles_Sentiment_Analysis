Story highlights NASA spacecraft, once thought junk, gets new lifeKepler has now found another exoplanet -- a planet outside our solar systemNew planet is 2.5 times the diameter of Earth, orbits star 180 light-years away(CNN) Kepler is still on the beat.The NASA spacecraft, which is designed to seek out other worlds but hit a snag when parts failed last year, found another planet thanks to some nifty repurposing by the space agency and its partners, NASA confirmed Thursday.The recently found world is named HIP 116454b and has a diameter 2.5 times the size of Earth's, said NASA in a press release. That would make it smaller than Neptune, the next-largest planet in our solar system.HIP 116454b orbits its star, which is in the direction of the constellation Pisces, in nine days and is too close to support life as we know it, the agency added.
