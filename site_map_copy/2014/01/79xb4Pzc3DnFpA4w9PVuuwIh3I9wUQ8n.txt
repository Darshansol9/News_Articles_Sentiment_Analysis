Story highlights The Andrews family runs a mom and pop pot shop in DenverFirst day of recreational pot sales equaled a month's sales of medical marijuanaColorado's recreational pot stores saw about $1 million in sales on first dayIn a far corner of downtown Denver, the Andrews family runs an old-fashioned mom and pop (and daughter) shop, selling the biggest novelty of a new era: marijuana.Their cozy store is fraught with risk. Although Colorado's landmark law makes it legal to sell marijuana to those 21 or older, the feds still classify it as illegal. The Obama administration says it won't prosecute buyers and sellers under the state's law, which went into effect January 1.Nonetheless, banks won't service recreational pot stores out of fear of being prosecuted for money laundering. So, the Andrews' shop operates as an all-cash business.For all the talk about how weed is recreational, the reality is it's financial. In fact, the expected boon is Colorado's "green rush" -- new taxes for government and untold millions to be made by growers and merchants.
