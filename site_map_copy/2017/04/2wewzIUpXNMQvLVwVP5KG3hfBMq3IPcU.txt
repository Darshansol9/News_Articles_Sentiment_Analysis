(CNN) A decades long chapter of injustice is finally closing in Florida.In 1949, four black men -- Charles Greenlee, Walter Irvin, Samuel Shepherd and Ernest Thomas -- were accused of raping Norma Padgett, a 17-year-old white girl.There were doubts about Padgett's testimony from the onset, but in the era of Jim Crow, a jury convicted the men without evidence of a crime.They came to be known as the "Groveland Four."And on Tuesday, after more than 60 years, the Florida House issued them a posthumous apology.Read More
