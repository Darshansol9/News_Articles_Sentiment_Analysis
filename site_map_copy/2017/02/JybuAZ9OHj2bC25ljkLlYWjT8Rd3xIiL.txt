Story highlights Curtis Reeves, 74, claims he acted in self-defenseReeves had complained about the other man texting in a movie theater(CNN) Three years after a dispute over texting in a movie theater left a man dead, a "Stand Your Ground" hearing is underway for a former Tampa, Florida, police captain charged with murder.The outcome of this hearing will determine whether Curtis Reeves, 74, stands trial for second-degree murder. He also could be cleared completely.Reeves has maintained that he acted in self-defense in the January 2014 killing of Chad Oulson, 43, during a screening of "Lone Survivor" in suburban Tampa. Reeves says that after he complained about Oulson's texting, the other man threw popcorn and a cell phone at him.Curtis Reeves, shown in court in 2014, is accused of killing Chad Oulson in a movie theater.Florida's "Stand Your Ground" law allows people to use deadly force when they fear death or great bodily harm.Prosecutors and defense attorneys faced off in court Monday.
