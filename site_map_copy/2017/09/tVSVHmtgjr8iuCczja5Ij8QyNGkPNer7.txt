(CNN) Hurricane Maria became the strongest storm to make landfall in Puerto Rico in 85 years when it barreled ashore Wednesday morning as a Category 4.In 1932, the San Ciprián hurricane made landfall in late September, pummeling the island for eight long hours.At the time, the population of Puerto Rico was a little more than 1.5 million people.Today, more than 3.3 million people call the US territory home.San Ciprián, with winds of 145 mph, ripped across the entire island from east to west.
