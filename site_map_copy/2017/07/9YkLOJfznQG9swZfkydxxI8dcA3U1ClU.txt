(CNN) A painter who loved to fly, a father who married his high school sweetheart, and a former football star who was living out his dream of being in the Navy. These were among the 16 service members killed in Monday's military plane crash in Mississippi, about 100 miles north of Jackson. The cause of the crash is still under investigation.In a somber procession Friday, 16 white hearses drove silently down a tree-lined street carrying the service members to the Air National Guard Base in Jackson."On behalf of the Marine Corps Reserve, I extend my deepest sympathies to the loved ones of those who perished... the Marines and sailor involved in this incident were among our finest," said Lt. Gen. Rex C. McMillian in a statement. "They dedicated their lives to our core values of honor, courage and commitment.
