(CNN) Officials at Boeing are investigating reports of a noose being found at the assembly plant in North Charleston, South Carolina.The "racially-charged symbol" -- as it was first referred -- was found at the plant Thursday, according to CNN affiliate WCSC . Officials later confirmed to CNN that the "symbol" was a noose.Brad Zaback, vice president and general manager of Boeing's program where 787 Dreamliner passenger jets are built, released a statement Friday condemning the incident."I am saddened and angered that a racially-charged symbol was discovered on site," Zaback's statement read. "Diversity and inclusion are core to Boeing's enduring values, and there is absolutely no place for racism and these cowardly acts in society and especially in our company."Zaback's statement also said this type of behavior wouldn't be tolerated and the company takes "these matters very seriously."Read More
