(CNN) Racing resumed Friday at Santa Anita Park after 22 horses died this season.The famed track near Los Angeles was closed to racing March 5.On Thursday, the California Horse Racing Board limited the use of whips and approved changes to medication rules. Park owners and the Thoroughbred Owners of California had agreed earlier "to protect the safety and welfare of horses and riders in the state," said a statement from the Stronach Group, the park's owner.Santa Anita Park was shut down earlier this month after Let's Light the Way had to be euthanized.Since late December, 22 horses have died at Santa Anita in Arcadia, California. Ten died during the same period last year.There were no horse deaths on Friday when racing resumed, Mike Marten from the California Horse Racing Board told CNN.Read More
