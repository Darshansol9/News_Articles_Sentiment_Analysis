(CNN) The man who has been taken into custody in connection with the shooting death of the reputed New York crime boss Francesco "Frank" Cali, will waive extradition on Monday, his lawyer told CNN.Anthony Comello, 24, who is being held in a New Jersey jail, will be formally charged with murder when he's extradited to the borough of Staten Island, where the killing happened, NYPD Chief of Detectives Dermot Shea said at a Saturday news conference.Comello's lawyer, Robert C. Gottlieb, said that his client will waive extradition when he appears in Ocean County court in New Jersey on Monday."The people who know him best, his family and friends, cannot believe what they are hearing," Gottlieb said in a statement Sunday.
