(CNN) Lawyers for New England Patriots owner Robert Kraft filed a second amended motion in court Wednesday to stop the public release of video evidence collected as part of a Florida prostitution sting.The motion argues that video evidence showing Kraft receiving "paid acts" in a room at a Florida day spa, could "destroy any prospect" of a fair trial.Kraft is one of more than a dozen defendants charged in a sting at the Orchids of Asia Day Spa in Jupiter, Florida. He has been charged with two counts of solicitation and has denied through a spokesman that he committed a crime.Kraft waived his arraignment and has requested a jury trial in the case.Florida prosecutors offered to drop misdemeanor charges against him in exchange for fines, community service and an admission that he would be found guilty if he went to trial.
