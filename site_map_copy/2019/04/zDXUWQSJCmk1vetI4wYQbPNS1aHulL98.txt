(CNN) The parents of a slain University of South Carolina student say they're on a mission to try to prevent tragedies when ordering rideshares.Seymour and Marci Josephson told ABC's "Good Morning America" on Monday they're glad the South Carolina House passed a bill that would require rideshare vehicles to display illuminated signsThe bill was named after their daughter, USC senior Samantha Josephson, who was killed after getting into a car she thought was an Uber."I think that's great. It's a great start," Seymour Josephson told GMA.But they want to see more action -- like adding QR codes or bar codes on vehicle windows that customers can scan, or requiring rideshare cars to have front license plates so customers can see if the correct car is headed toward them.Read More
