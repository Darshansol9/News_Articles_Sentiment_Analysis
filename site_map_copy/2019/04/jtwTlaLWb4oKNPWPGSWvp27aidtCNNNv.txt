(CNN) "Abraham Lincoln died this morning at 22 minutes after seven."Ten words that changed the course of history. It was written on a telegram on April 15, 1865 -- the morning after Lincoln was shot by John Wilkes Booth at Ford's Theatre in Washington, D.C.The telegram, which notified the public of the President's death, goes up for sale Monday , the anniversary of that date."This document was the official word to the nation that the President had died," said Nathan Raab, the Raab Collection's president. "It is truly one of our great finds."The Raab Collection is a historical documents dealer.
