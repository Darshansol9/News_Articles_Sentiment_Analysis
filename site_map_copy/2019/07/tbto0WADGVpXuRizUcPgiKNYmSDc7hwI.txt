(CNN) A judge in Hawaii denied a request for a temporary restraining order that would have prevented scientists from building another telescope on Mauna Kea.Judge Greg Nakamura denied the request Tuesday although the court has not yet issued a written order on the matter, Deputy Hawaii Attorney General Bryan Yee told CNN.This comes after days of protests at the site on Hawaii's Big Island, which some native Hawaiians consider sacred.Hundreds of protesters, or protectors as they prefer to be called, have spent a week blocking construction from starting on the Thirty-Meter Telescope at the top of the mountain. The device has been touted as the world's most sophisticated telescope.Mauna Kea is already home to 13 observatories, as the high elevation of 6,632 feet and dark skies make for perfect space watching.Read More
