(CNN) Investigators found "multiple items of evidence" while searching a Salt Lake City home in connection with the disappearance of missing college student Mackenzie Lueck, according to police.Authorities have spoken with the owner of the residence in the Fairpark neighborhood, Salt Lake City Police Chief Mike Brown told reporters Thursday. Brown did not identify the homeowner, who is not in police custody.Lueck, a 23-year-old senior at the University of Utah, has been missing since the morning of June 17, when she was dropped off by a Lyft in a park where she met another individual, according to police.Investigators are looking for a mattress and box spring that were given away from the Fairpark home last week, Brown said. He asked that whoever took them contact the Salt Lake City Police Department.Brown added investigators are examining the case's "digital footprint."Read More
