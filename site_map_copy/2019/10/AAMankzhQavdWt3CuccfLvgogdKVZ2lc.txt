(CNN) Missouri's state health director, Dr. Randall Williams, told officials at a state hearing that he tracked the menstrual periods of women who visited Planned Parenthood with a spreadsheet, the Kansas City Star reported Tuesday.The spreadsheet, compiled at Williams' request by the state's main inspector, was used to identify patients who had undergone failed abortions, according to the Star.CNN has reached out to the Missouri State Health Department and Williams for a response.Williams' testimony was part of an ongoing hearing in which the St. Louis Planned Parenthood clinic is fighting to continue performing abortions after the state refused to renew its license, which was supposed to expire on May 31. Testimony is expected to continue this week.The state's last abortion clinic , Reproductive Health Services of Planned Parenthood of the St. Louis Region, remains open.
