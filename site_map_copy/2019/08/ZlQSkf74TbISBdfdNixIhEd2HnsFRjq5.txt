(CNN) The Federal Aviation Administration is investigating after a pilot allegedly fell asleep at the controls while transporting a patient on a Boston MedFlight in June, the agency said.The helicopter was flying from Martha's Vineyard to a Boston hospital on June 24, FAA spokesman Rick Breitenfeldt said.The pilot overflew the designated helipad while transporting the patient, Boston MedFlight CEO Maura Hughes said in a statement.No one was injured during the incident and the transport was successfully completed, Hughes said.Hughes said fatigue played a role in the incident but the medical transport company is now working with a safety consultant to review policies and procedures.Read More
