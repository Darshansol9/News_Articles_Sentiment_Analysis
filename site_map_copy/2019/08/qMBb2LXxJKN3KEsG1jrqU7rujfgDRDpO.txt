(CNN) When the El Paso Walmart where 22 people were killed reopens in three to four months, the renovated property will have a memorial to the shooting victims, a company spokesman said Wednesday.The memorial is in the early planning stages and the company is seeking input from the community on the design, Randy Hargrove, Walmart's senior director for national media relations, said.Walmart has also been talking with employees about how to handle the memorial."Our goal is to establish a memorial that honors the victims, recognizes the binational relationship between El Paso and Ciudad Juarez, and celebrates the strength of the El Paso community," Hargrove said.Hargrove said Walmart will take the store "down to the shell and is basically rebuilding it."Read More
