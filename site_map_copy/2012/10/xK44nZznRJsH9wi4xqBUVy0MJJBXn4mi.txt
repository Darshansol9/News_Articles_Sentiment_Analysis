By Moni Basu, CNN(CNN) -- On a September day this year, Courtney Pearson stood anxiously on the steps of the Lyceum, the famed old Greek Revival building on the University of Mississippi campus.There, she learned she was elected homecoming queen. There, she stood as the first black woman to hold that title at Ole Miss.Five decades before, James Meredith had entered the Lyceum as the university's first black student. He risked his life as he walked inside, his admission a milestone in the struggle for integration that sparked deadly riots on campus.As anniversary observances of that pivotal day came to a close on the Oxford campus, Pearson, 21, took her place as queen.
