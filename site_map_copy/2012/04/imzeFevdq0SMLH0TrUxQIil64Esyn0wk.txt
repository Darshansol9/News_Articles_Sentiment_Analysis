By Alan Duke, CNNLos Angeles (CNN) -- Acura apologized Wednesday for a casting document that called for an African-American actor who was "not too dark" in the car company's Super Bowl ad.The controversial casting call document, published Wednesday by the celebrity gossip site TMZ , "pulls back the curtain" on the widely known practice of casting based on skin tone in Hollywood, a longtime manager said.TMZ reported that it got the audition posting from "an African-American actor who didn't fit the profile, and who's pissed."The role was for an "African-American Car Dealer" who would appear in a car showroom scene with Jerry Seinfeld. Jay Leno also appeared in the ad.
