Story highlights Carl Koppelman creates portraits of unidentified deceased peopleOne family positively identified a relative after seeing one of Koppelman's reconstructions(CNN) At first glance, Carl Koppelman's portraits might seem innocuous.But a single somber thread connects the computer-generated faces staring back from his screen: They are all reconstructions of unidentified dead people.Since he lost his job as an accountant for Disney in 2009, the 53-year-old amateur painter from Los Angeles has been illustrating deceased Jane and John Does -- and helping family members identify them."I've always had the ability to draw faces," he says.
