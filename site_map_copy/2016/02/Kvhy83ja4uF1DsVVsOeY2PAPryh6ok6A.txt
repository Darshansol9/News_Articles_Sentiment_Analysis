Story highlights Five teenagers were charged in connection with a woman's alleged gang rapeBrooklyn District Attorney Ken Thompson cites a "lack of reliable evidence"(CNN) Charges have been dropped against five teenagers connected with a woman's alleged gang rape at gunpoint in a Brooklyn playground, Brooklyn District Attorney Ken Thompson announced on Wednesday.He cited a "lack of reliable evidence" and said the woman and her father had "provided multiple inconsistent accounts."One of the teenagers pulled a gun and demanded the father leave, police have said. Each of the five alleged assailants were then said to have raped the woman."That night, this young woman's father and the five young men engaged in conduct that was reprehensible and wrong, but because of the lack of reliable evidence, criminal charges simply cannot be sustained," Thompson said in a statement.Read More
