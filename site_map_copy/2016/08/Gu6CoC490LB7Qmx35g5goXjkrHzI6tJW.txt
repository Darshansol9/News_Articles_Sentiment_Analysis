Story highlights African-American cop reacts to Dallas sniper attackAnwar Sanders says he feels like a victim of both sidesWe are at a breaking point, says SandersNew York (CNN) Anwar Sanders is a police officer in Santa Fe, New Mexico. He's also a young black man.And, Sanders says, after a week where the world saw two videos of African-American men shot by police, followed by a sniper attack in Dallas that left five police officers dead, he feels "like a victim of both sides.""I feel for the families. I live both those lives," an emotional Sanders said in an interview Friday with Don Lemon on CNN's Newsroom.For Sanders, it doesn't matter if he's in or out of uniform. He doesn't feel safe."This morning, I woke up and I was afraid for my safety ... jumping into my patrol car to go to work. I was afraid," he said.Read More
