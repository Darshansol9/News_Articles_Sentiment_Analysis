(CNN) The US Border Patrol's El Paso Sector had planned a "crowd control" exercise on Election Day near the port of entry in the hometown of Democratic Rep. Beto O'Rourke, who's challenging Republican Sen. Ted Cruz.But it was postponed, according to agency spokeswoman Denisse Licon.The abrupt postponement came after the American Civil Liberties Union and others questioned the timing of the exercise in a Hispanic enclave near the Paso del Norte Port of Entry."It shouldn't have taken outrage by us, congresspeople and the community for Border Patrol to know this would cause serious problems on Election Day," Terri Burke, executive director for the ACLU of Texas, said in a statement."These suspicious exercises scheduled in a Latinx neighborhood raise serious concerns about whether this was intended to intimidate Texans from exercising their right to vote," she said.Read More
