(CNN) Going to a police station to file a domestic violence report is difficult enough. But imagine trying to do it with three small children in tow.That's the challenge a mother faced earlier this month when she showed up at the West Jordan, Utah, Police Department.But the mom got some welcome -- and unexpected -- emergency child care when an officer offered to look after her kids while she talked to a victim advocate and filled out paperwork.Records clerks snapped a photo of Officer Robert Lofgran cradling the woman's baby. Since the West Jordan Police Department posted it on Facebook it's been widely shared and attracted thousands of likes."It isn't easy for women to seek help in escaping an abusive situation at home ..." said one Facebook commenter.
