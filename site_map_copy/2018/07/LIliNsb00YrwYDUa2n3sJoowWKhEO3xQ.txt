(CNN) A Brazilian mother and her 10-year-old son, who were separated as part of the Trump administration's zero tolerance policy on illegal migration, were reunited on Thursday in Chicago.Sirley Silveira Paixao arrived to Chicago to pick up her son after a judge ordered that the boy be released to her care, her attorney, Britt Miller, said.The mother and son were all smiles as they walked up to a tangle of microphones to address reporters. Paixao leaned over and kissed her son on the cheek.Through a translator, Paixao said in a press conference following the reunion that she was "extremely happy."Paixao said she was looking forward to taking her son to Massachusetts, where she has been living with family friends, getting him enrolled in school and getting herself a job.Read More
