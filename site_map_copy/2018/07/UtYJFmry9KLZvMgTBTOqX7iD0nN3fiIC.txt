(CNN) An undocumented immigrant who was stopped while delivering food to a military base in Brooklyn, New York, has been released from detention and his deportation proceedings have been paused, the man's attorneys said Tuesday.Pablo Villavicencio, 35, was detained by military police in June at Fort Hamilton while delivering an order from the brick-oven pizza restaurant in Queens where he worked. He was handed over to Immigration and Customs Enforcement.He was set free from the Hudson County Correctional Center in Kearny, New Jersey, on Thursday night."Thank you. Thank you for everything," he said exuberantly while coming close to tears. "I'm so happy."And, according to a CNN translation, he added in Spanish: "I love this country. I love this country because it gave me this family. (It) gave me my daughters, but I do not share the intolerance and disrespect of the current government."Read More
