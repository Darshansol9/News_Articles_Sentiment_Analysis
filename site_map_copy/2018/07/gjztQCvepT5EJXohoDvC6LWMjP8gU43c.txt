(CNN) As gunshots rang out and glass shattered all around, staffers at the Capital Gazette cowered under desks, fearing for their lives.Except Wendi Winters. A witness said she stood up, grabbed a trash can and a recycling bin, and charged the gunman.For her heroism, the 65-year-old woman paid with her life. But several staffers at the Annapolis, Maryland, newspaper say she saved theirs.Survivors of last month's attack shared the account of that day in a story the Capital ran over the weekend.Eleven employees were in the office that day.
