(CNN) Nearly two weeks after spring began, residents in the northeastern United States trudged through another snowstorm Monday morning.New York City experienced the eighth snowiest April day in the city's history. About 4.8 inches of snow fell in Central Park on Monday morning, well above the average April monthly snow total of 0.6 inches.A lone man walks in the snow on the Brooklyn Promenade on Monday.The last time that at least 4 inches fell in Central Park was back on April 7, 2003 -- about 15 years ago. LaGuardia Airport at one point recorded 2 inches of snow per hour.New York City and Philadelphia had been under advisories for as much as 6 inches of heavy, wet snow on Monday morning.A worker adjusts the awning to protect fruits and vegetables sitting outside at his produce store on Atlantic Avenue in Brooklyn on Monday.The New York Yankees postponed their hotly anticipated home opener because of the snowy weather, and the game was rescheduled for Tuesday at 4:05 pm ET.Men shovel snow from the sidewalks in front of Yankee Stadium on Monday.
