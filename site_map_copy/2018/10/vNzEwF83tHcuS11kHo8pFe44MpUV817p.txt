(CNN) These kinds of limos have worried safety experts for years: regular SUVs that have been jacked up, stretched out and converted into limousines.It's unclear whether the limo's modification or some other factor contributed to the mass tragedy. In a statement Monday, Prestige Limousine Chauffeur Service, which operated the vehicle, said it is "performing a detailed internal investigation to determine the cause of the accident and the steps we can take in order to prevent future accidents."Still, officials say it's time to examine whether regulations on limousines are sufficient."This does need to be a wake-up call," said Robert Sumwalt, chairman of the National Transportation Safety Board.
