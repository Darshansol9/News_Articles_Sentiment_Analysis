Florence, South Carolina (CNN) Three deputies were going to his house to investigate a sexual assault case allegedly involving his son. When they got out of their car, Frederick Hopkins shot them, officials say.Hopkins, 74, is facing murder and attempted murder charges after authorities say he shot seven law enforcement officers Wednesday in Florence, South Carolina."These officers were ambushed very quickly," said Richland County Sheriff Leon Lott, whose office is conducting the investigation. "As soon as they got there, the ambush was in place."The three Florence County sheriff's deputies went to Hopkins' house to interview his adopted son, 28-year-old Seth Hopkins, as part of an investigation into criminal sexual assault, Lott said.They called ahead and knew someone inside the home was expecting them.
