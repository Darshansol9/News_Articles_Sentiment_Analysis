(CNN) A rabbi who was inside Tree of Life synagogue when a gunman stormed the building said Monday that he tried to help worshipers escape -- once he realized the noise he heard was gunfire.Rabbi Jeffrey Myers told CNN's Alisyn Camerota that Saturday was a "typical Shabbat morning" when he began services for one of three congregations meeting in the Pittsburgh synagogue.When he heard loud banging, he first thought the noise was a a metal coat rack that had fallen over, Myers said."I wasn't immediately concerned," he said -- until he saw people from other parts of the synagogue running down stairs."Another round of what I now knew was gunfire came out," he said.
