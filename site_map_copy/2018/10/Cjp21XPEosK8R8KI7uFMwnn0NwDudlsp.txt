(CNN) A visit from a drag queen who participated in a literacy event at a school in Colorado sparked outrage from some parents. But the school is standing by its choice.The event was hosted last week by Rocky Top Middle School in Thornton, Colorado. Guests were invited by the school to talk about their jobs and the connection of literacy to their careers.Dancer and performance artist Zachary Sullivan, whose stage name is Jessica L'Whor, told CNN on Wednesday he was invited to the event after a fan who attends the school suggested him as a guest.Sullivan, who is gay, was dressed in character and said he introduced himself as Miss Jessica, deciding to drop part of his stage name because he was in a school environment.
