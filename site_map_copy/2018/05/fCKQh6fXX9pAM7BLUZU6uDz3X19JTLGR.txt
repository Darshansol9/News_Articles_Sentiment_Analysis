Pahoa, Hawaii (CNN) Steve Gebbie has to wear a face mask every time he comes close to his home on Hawaii's Big Island.When the Kilauea volcano erupted May 3, Gebbie and hundreds of others were forced from their homes in the Leilani Estates subdivision. His home is still standing, but it's surrounded by toxic gas."It's hard to believe that it's happened in our neighborhood," Gebbie said. "And every day has gotten worse."Residents have periodically been allowed to go back and check on their homes.Fissures , or cracks in the ground, are opening wider and wider by the day.
