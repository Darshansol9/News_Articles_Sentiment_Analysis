(CNN) A 15-year-old boy accused of killing two students in a shooting at a Kentucky high school last month will be tried as an adult, state police said Friday.A grand jury indicted Gabriel R. Parker on two counts of murder and 14 counts of first-degree assault in the January 23 shooting at Marshall County High School in western Kentucky, police said in a statement.He was arraigned Friday at the Marshall County Circuit Court. His bond was set at $1.5 million, according to The Courier-Journal in Louisville.Before the arraignment, Parker was held in a juvenile detention facility and had not been identified.In Kentucky, a suspect can be charged in an adult court if a judge rules the case meets certain criteria, officials said.Read More
