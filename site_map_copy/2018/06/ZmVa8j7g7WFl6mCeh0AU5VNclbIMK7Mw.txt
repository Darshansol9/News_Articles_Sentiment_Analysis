(CNN) Rosalyn Haber remembers it vividly: Uniformed men lining up women and children and then yanking her mother away from her.Haber was 13 at the time, a prisoner at the Auschwitz death camp."I want to go with my mother! I want to go with my mother!" she remembered crying.But she knew she'd never see her mother again.
