(CNN) Rusty and Jenny Perry fled their home in Hawaii's Big island last month to escape bubbling lava following the Kilauea volcano eruption.The couple grows lychee, bananas and papaya on their farm in Kapoho. When they evacuated, they missed shipments of produce to other parts of the state. And with that came lost income."It's just waiting there, waiting to be picked," Jenny Perry told CNN affiliate Hawaii News Now.But even as their produce awaits, they've been sneaking onto the farm to salvage what they can.
