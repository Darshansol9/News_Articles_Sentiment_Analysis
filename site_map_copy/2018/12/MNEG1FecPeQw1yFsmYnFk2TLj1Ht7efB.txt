(CNN) The parents of the 16-year-old black varsity wrestler who was forced to cut his dreadlocks before a match have spoken out for the first time since the incident.Charles and Rosa Johnson, speaking through their attorney, Dominic A. Speziali, said in a Monday statement that the conduct of the referee who forced their son, Andrew, to cut his hair "appears more egregious as additional information comes to light."They allege that the referee was late to the meet and didn't question their son's hair or the need for a head covering during the initial evaluation.The referee did not immediately respond to CNN's requests for comment Tuesday afternoon.The referee later told Andrew his hair and headgear were not in compliance with league regulations, Speziali said in the statement.Read More
