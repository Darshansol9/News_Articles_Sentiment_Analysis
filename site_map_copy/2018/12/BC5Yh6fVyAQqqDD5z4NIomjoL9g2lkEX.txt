(CNN) It's back to the drawing board for University of North Carolina officials looking for a new location for the "Silent Sam" Confederate monument.The Board of Governors rejected a plan to build a $5.3 million home for the controversial statue, university spokesperson Carly Miller told CNN on Friday.The board gave administrators until March 15 to develop another recommendation.Board chairman Harry Smith told CNN Raleigh affiliate WTVD that the university rejected the plan because of student safety and the price."The $5.3 million is, I think, pretty tough for a lot of us to swallow," he said.Read More
