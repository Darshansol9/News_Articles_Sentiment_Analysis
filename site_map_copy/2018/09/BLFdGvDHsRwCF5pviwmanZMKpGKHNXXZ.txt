(CNN) A former employee for Southwest Airlines has filed a discrimination lawsuit against the carrier, alleging it allowed employees to create a "whites-only" break room at Houston's Hobby Airport.In a lawsuit filed Wednesday in federal court, Jamel Parker said the break room was used for years until recent airport renovations turned it into a supervisor's office.Parker also alleged he was fired from the airline unfairly due to his race, and he is seeking an unspecified amount in damages.A pretrial and scheduling conference is set for January 25 in Houston.Southwest Airlines said it does not comment on litigation matters but said it's an equal opportunity employer and puts people first.Read More
