(CNN) New images from NOAA are giving evacuees a chance to check on their property from afar.NOAA's new "high-definition aerial photos" were taken by planes equipped with cameras taking the images from less than a mile above the devastation. They tell CNN that the images are vital to observing which areas are the hardest hit and where the help needs to go after storms."The primary purpose of this imagery is to support emergency response efforts," NOAA spokesperson Keeley Belva told CNN. "Having it available more broadly allows people to check on their property from afar and stay out of harm's way."Residents can check their imagery by going to NOAA's website hereThe clarity of the images allows residents to not only see if their houses are still standing, but even if there is potential roof damage like missing shingles.Read More
