The robbery was reported to local authorities shortly after it happened last Thursday night, and local media said no arrests had been made as of Monday.A law enforcement official said the evidence so far indicates the incident was "a crime of opportunity," and that police have seen nothing so far to indicate Breyer was targeted for robbery.The U.S. Marshals Service provides protection for members of the high court when they are traveling, and agency spokesman Jeff Carter said Monday that the marshals service "is aware of the incident involving Justice Breyer on the Caribbean island of Nevis and is assisting the Supreme Court Police and local law enforcement authorities with the investigation as needed." Souter suffered some minor bruises and was briefly treated then released from a local hospital.Justice Byron White was attacked in July 1982 while giving a speech in Utah.
