Story highlights DREAMer Daniela Vargas could be deported as early as Friday, lawyers sayThey are working to prepare a package with personal statements about Vargas from people who know her(CNN) Fighting to keep 22-year-old DREAMer Daniela Vargas from deportation, her lawyers are in a race against time.They prepared special documents Thursday night, hoping to stop the expedited deportation of Vargas, which could come as early as Friday.Vargas came into the United States on a visa waiver program. Because of the stipulations of that program she could be deported without a hearing in front of a federal immigration judge.Her attorneys hope a package with personal statements about Vargas from people who know her lands in sympathetic hands, and she is granted a trial instead of immediate deportation.The attorneys also are seeking a stay of removal so Vargas can submit a renewal application for the Obama-era Deferred Action for Childhood Arrivals program, known as DACA.Read More
