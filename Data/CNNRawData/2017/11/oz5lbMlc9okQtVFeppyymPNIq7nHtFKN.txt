(CNN) It's been six weeks since Maria left Puerto Rico in shambles. The hurricane-ravaged island is still contending with widespread power and water shortages, and dozens of stores remain closed.But for employees of three stores, being without work hasn't meant being without a paycheck.TJX Companies, which owns TJ Maxx, Marshalls and HomeGoods, has continued to pay its employees.A company spokeswoman confirmed the payments to CNN after she was asked about a Facebook post that's been shared more than 41,000 times."We believe it is the right thing for us to do under these circumstances," TJX spokeswoman Erika Tower said in an emailed statement.Read More
