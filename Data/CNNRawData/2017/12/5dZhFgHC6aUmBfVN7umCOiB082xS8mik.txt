(CNN) It remains among America's most heinous nightmares: the lives of 20 little children -- dancers and swimmers, pianists and painters, budding readers, little sisters and big brothers -- extinguished in a flash of violence inside Sandy Hook Elementary.Taken with them on that chilly Friday in December -- just 11 days before Christmas -- were six adults felled by the same gunman as they refused to abandon their sacred trust to safeguard the smallest among them.Five years later, even those who have never set foot near Newtown, Connecticut, can conjure the scene painted by police of a first-grade classroom transformed into a killing field. Can see the faces of anguished parents desperate for proof of life, then later, tiny caskets overloaded with stuffed animals never to be named.Since the 2012 massacre, a new school has been built for the students of a town known, now and for years to come, as a cradle of sorrow -- but also as the home of quiet resilience and untold love.The 12 girls, eight boys and six women whose futures were stolen that day will be remembered, always.
