Story highlights Virginia governor says white supremacists divide AmericaMore than 100 marches around US on Sunday nightCharlottesville, Virginia (CNN) Virginia Gov. Terry McAuliffe, speaking Sunday at a prayer rally in Charlottesville, renewed his calls for white supremacists to leave the city in the wake of violence that saw one person killed and troopers assigned to the governor's travel detail killed in a helicopter crash.McAuliffe denounced the people who had come to this college town for a "Unite the Right" rally, saying they weren't the patriots they make themselves out to be."They get out of bed every day to hate people and divide our country," McAuliffe said.On Saturday, McAuliffe told the demonstrators to go home. On Sunday he went further."Let's be honest, they need to leave America, because they are not Americans," he said.
