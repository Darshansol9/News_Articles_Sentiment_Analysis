Story highlights Photographer Helge Skodvin documented the World Taxidermy ChampionshipThe taxidermists try to make the animals as lifelike as possible(CNN) Lions and tigers and ... Bigfoot? Photographer Helge Skodvin saw it all last month at the World Taxidermy Championship.More than 300 of the world's best taxidermists -- from 47 states and 14 countries -- gathered in Springfield, Missouri, to showcase their work, which included stuffed mammals, birds, fish and yes, even a Bigfoot concept.While documenting the taxidermy, Skodvin said he focused on making each photo "as clean and simple as possible."At first glance, his photos may appear to be quite humorous and amusing. Take, for example, the second photo in the gallery above."There is a man driving his big truck, and in the back seat there is a stuffed deer putting its head up," Skodvin said.
