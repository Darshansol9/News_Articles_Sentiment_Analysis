Story highlights Princess Anne police chief won't comment when asked if carbon monoxide was a causeSeven children, whose ages ranged from 6 to 16, were found deadAuthorities say a generator inside the home was providing electricity(CNN) Eight members of a Maryland family were found dead in a house Monday when police went to check on the father, who had failed to show up for work.The man and seven children, whose ages ranged from 6 to 16, were discovered in the home, Princess Anne Police Chief Scott Keller said.No foul play is suspected in the deaths, he said, adding that everyone in the house was related.The electricity in the home was powered by a generator, the chief said. He wouldn't comment when asked if the deaths were related to carbon monoxide poisoning."We have some ideas (about what happened)," Keller said.
