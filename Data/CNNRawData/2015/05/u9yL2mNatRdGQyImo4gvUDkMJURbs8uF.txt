Story highlights Ana is downgraded to a tropical depressionIt made landfall at 6:00 a.m. just north of Myrtle Beach, South CarolinaAna is the first named storm of the 2015 Atlantic tropical storm season(CNN) The first Atlantic tropical storm of the year weakened as it made landfall in South Carolina early Sunday.Ana is now a tropical depression, the National Hurricane Center said in its 2 p.m. ET update. Warnings and watches were lifted.As a tropical storm, Ana had sustained winds at 50 mph. When it came ashore just north of Myrtle Beach at 6 a.m. Sunday, winds were at 45 mph, the hurricane center said."On the forecast track, the center of Ana will move over eastern North Carolina later today and tonight," the center said.
