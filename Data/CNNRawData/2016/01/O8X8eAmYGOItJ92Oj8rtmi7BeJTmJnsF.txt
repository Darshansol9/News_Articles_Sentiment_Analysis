(CNN) NASA's first in-flight tragedy decades ago brings back haunting memories.January 28 marks three decades since the space shuttle Challenger disaster claimed the lives of its entire crew.On board were seven team members who perished in the wreck, mission specialists Ellison S. Onizuka, Ron McNair and Judith Resnick, payload specialist Greg Jarvis, pilot Mike Smith, commander Dick Scobee, and Christa McAuliffe, who was to be the first civilian and teacher in space.There are many who can still recall where they were when the shuttle exploded -- the result of a failure in the seals on one of the solid rocket boosters, it was later determined.In many ways, the Space Shuttle Challenger disaster was like President John F. Kennedy's assassination in 1963 or the terror attacks of September 11, 2001. It was a visceral moment for many Americans, and decades later it remains a tragedy that the country continues to remember.Read More
