Story highlights So-called Rooney Rule requires NFL teams to interview a diverse group of candidatesThe rule will expand to women for all executive positions, NFL chief saysRoger Goodell announces the move at first NFL Women's Summit in San FranciscoSan Francisco (CNN) The NFL's push for diversity is growing.The rule -- named after Pittsburgh Steelers owner Dan Rooney -- requires that NFL teams interview a diverse group of candidates. For example, teams are required to interview a minority candidate whenever there is a head coach opening."We're going to make that commitment, and we're going to formalize that we as a league are going to do that for women as well as in all of our executive positions," Goodell said.Goodell kicked off the two-day event in San Francisco with references to his twin 14-year-old daughters and 11 nieces.Read More
