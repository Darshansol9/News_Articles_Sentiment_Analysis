By Chris Boyette, CNNNew York (CNN) -- Police are investigating the slaying of a 32-year-old man in the Greenwich Village neighborhood early Saturday as a hate crime because the gunman made multiple anti-gay comments, they said.It is at least the fourth violent attack in two weeks believed to be motivated by anti-gay bias, police said.The suspect's anti-gay remarks were noted before the shooting took place, Police Commissioner Raymond Kelly said. The man was seen urinating on the street outside a bar before going inside and making anti-gay comments to the bartender and brandishing a silver handgun.A little after midnight, the gunman and two other companions confronted the victim, Marc Carson, and another man he was with on the street.
