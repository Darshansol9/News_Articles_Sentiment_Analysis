(CNN) Nine police officers were injured in an explosion late Wednesday when they responded to a home in North Haven, Connecticut, to investigate a report of domestic violence, authorities said.A body was later found inside a structure at the scene, but authorities have not identified the remains, State Trooper Kelly Grant said in a news conference Thursday morning. The medical examiner will conduct an autopsy to determine the identity and cause of death.All of the officers remained hospitalized Thursday morning, according to North Haven Police Deputy Chief Jonathan Mulhern. They were part of the South Central Regional SWAT Team, which is made up of officers from six Connecticut communities.Of the officers injured, Mulhern said, five were from East Haven, three were from North Haven and one was from Branford. All suffered injuries "that were consistent with the blast or an explosion," Mulhern said.Yale New Haven Hospital told CNN it received nine patients from last night's incident, and that all of them were in fair condition Thursday morning.Read More
