(CNN) The top flutist at the Boston Symphony Orchestra is suing the group because she says she makes only 75% of what a male counterpart makes.Principal flutist Elizabeth Rowe filed the lawsuit July 2 -- the day after the Massachusetts Equal Pay Act went into effect.In the lawsuit, Rowe claims that she is paid less than the principal oboe, viola, trumpet, timpani and horns, which are all held by men.It says her responsibilities are "substantially similar" to principal oboist John Ferrillo.Elizabeth Rowe is the principal flutist at the Boston Symphony Orchestra."Both the principal oboe and principal flute are leaders of their woodwind sections, they are seated adjacent to each other, they each play with the Boston Symphony Chamber Players, and are both leaders of the orchestra in similarly demanding roles," the lawsuit says.Read More
