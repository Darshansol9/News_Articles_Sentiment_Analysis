(CNN) Motel 6 has reached a tentative agreement in a lawsuit that claims it discriminated against some of its Latino guests and violated their privacy, a civil rights group says.The suit says two Phoenix locations of the hotel chain gave details of at least seven guests' whereabouts and handed other personal information over to federal immigration agents before the guests got arrested.The claim was filed by a Latino civil rights group, the Mexican American Legal Defense and Educational Fund.The suit also alleges Motel 6 has a practice of giving guests' check-in information to US Immigration and Customs Enforcement without a warrant or a reasonable suspicion that criminal activity is going on. Turning over their information to ICE is a violation of their privacy, the suit says.Motel 6 has agreed to settle the suit, but it's subject to a federal judge's approval.Read More
