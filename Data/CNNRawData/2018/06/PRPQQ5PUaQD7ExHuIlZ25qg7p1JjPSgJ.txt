(CNN) Now, nearly a year after Linkin Park lead singer Chester Bennington took his own life, it's clear to his widow that in the days before he died, there were warning signs."I am now more educated about those signs, but they were definitely there: the hopelessness, the change of behavior, isolation," Talinda Bennington told CNN during an interview with CNN's Anderson Cooper during the CNN Town Hall, "Finding Hope: Battling America's Suicide Crisis."The program airs Sunday, June 24, at 7 p.m. ET.But, Bennington explained, these behaviors weren't uncommon for her husband, a father of six who had battled depression for a long time."That was all part of our daily life," she said.
