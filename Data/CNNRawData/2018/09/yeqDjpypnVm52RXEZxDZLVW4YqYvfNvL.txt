(CNN) They just wanted fries and to see some diversity on the posters at McDonald's, but their prank made them $50,000 richer.Jevh Maravilla, the college student who faked a promotional poster and hung it up at the fast-food restaurant in Pearland, Texas, along with his friend, Christian Toledo, were invited earlier this week to "The Ellen DeGeneres Show."And of course, Ellen being Ellen, she surprised them with a check for $25,000 each -- courtesy of McDonald's.Inspired by the amount of Asian diversity in the film "Crazy Rich Asians," the two friends spent a month and about $100 to create the poster."We wanted to be Crazy Middle Class (Asians)," Toledo told DeGeneres.Read More
