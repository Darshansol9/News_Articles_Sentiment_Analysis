(CNN) Water appears to have receded in some areas of the Carolinas, but more flooding from Hurricane Florence could arrive through the weekend, officials say.More than a dozen rivers in North and South Carolina already have overflowed their banks and the water -- particularly the Waccamaw River, which spans both states -- is expected to swell putting additional residents in harm's way. Officers were going door-to-door along the river in Brunswick County, North Carolina encouraging residents to evacuate.President Donald Trump met with first responders in Conway, South Carolina on Wednesday and warned them of the danger of rising rivers."It's going to get rough for South Carolina," he told them."You're going to have a rebuilding process, and we are behind you from day one," Trump said, calling this the "calm before the storm, because you're going to have a lot of water."Read More
