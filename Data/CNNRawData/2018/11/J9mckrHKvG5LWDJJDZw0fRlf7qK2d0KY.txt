(CNN) For months now, the Equal Rights Amendment has been on the brink of becoming federal law. Virginia might be the state to put it over the top.Jennifer Carroll Foy, a Virginia legislator, just wrapped a tour across the state campaigning for the ERA, which says that "Equality of rights under the law shall not be denied or abridged by the United States or by any state on account of sex."It was passed by Congress in 1972 but still has yet to be ratified by the states more than four decades later."The majority of places around the world have enshrined women's fundamental rights as people in their founding documents," Foy said. "The United States have failed to do that."For an amendment to be added to the Constitution, a minimum of 38 states have to sign off.
