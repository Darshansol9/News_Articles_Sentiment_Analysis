(CNN) Dale Earnhardt Jr. has made his first statement since surviving a plane crash.The famed former NASCAR driver and his wife were on a private jet Thursday that crashed into a fence at the end of a runway upon landing at Elizabethton Municipal Airport in east Tennessee, officials said."We are truly blessed that all on board escaped with no serious injuries, including our daughter, our two pilots and our dog Gus," Earnhardt said in a tweet Monday evening. "I am thankful for the quick response of my pilots, local law enforcement, emergency personnel and hospital staff."Earnhardt spent a few hours at a hospital before he was released.
