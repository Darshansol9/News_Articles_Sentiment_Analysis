(CNN) He designed a machine that makes water from the air. Now, after Hurricane Dorian, he's trying to bring it to the Bahamas.Moses West, of San Antonio, Texas, has brought water to Flint, Michigan, and Puerto Rico -- all through his Atmospheric Water Generation machines, which extract moisture from the atmosphere and turn it into water.The science behind it is similar to what many learned in elementary school. It's really just condensation -- the same thing that creates moisture when you breathe on a window, for example.It's just done on a much, much larger scale.And, if all goes well, he'll be bringing it to the Bahamas, where tens of thousands were left homeless after Hurricane Dorian, leaving some islands with no power or running water.
