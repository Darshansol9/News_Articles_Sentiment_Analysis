(CNN) The B-17 bomber that crashed Wednesday near Hartford, Connecticut, belonged to a nonprofit group that supports educational living history events.The vintage WWII-era aircraft was a civilian-registered aircraft with the Collings Foundation, which is based in Stow, Massachusetts. Its mission is to support events featuring aircraft and the "preservation, exhibition and interaction of historical artifacts," its website states.With more than two dozen historic aircraft, spanning the early 1900s to the Vietnam War, the foundation claims to operate "one of the world's greatest collections of historic aircraft," according to its site.It has completed a number of restoration projects and it has several underway.Read More
