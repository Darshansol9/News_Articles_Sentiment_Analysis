(CNN) Several days before Memorial Day, a Vietnam War memorial in Massachusetts has been vandalized with "hate-related" graffiti.Police are canvassing the area near the memorial in Boston's Dorchester neighborhood.Early Thursday, flags were ripped down and tossed; dozens of plants were torn from the ground; and stone monuments were marked with hateful graffiti -- including a swastika -- according to a news release from the Massachusetts State Police.In addition to graffiti, police say flags were torn down and plants were ripped from the ground.The memorial is on a space owned by the University of Massachusetts Boston and includes the names of 80 Vietnam War veterans, according to the university."The University of Massachusetts and the Massachusetts State Police condemn this despicable act and are conducting a thorough and coordinated investigation to determine who is responsible and to hold that person or persons accountable," the release said.Read More
