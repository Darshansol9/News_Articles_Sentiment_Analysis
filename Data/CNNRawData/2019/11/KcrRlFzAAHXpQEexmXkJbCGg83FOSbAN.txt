(CNN) Hundreds of people donated to pay off lunch debts at a Minnesota school after hearing that students' hot meals were thrown away.About 40 students at Richfield High School who had a lunch balance of at least $15 had their lunches thrown away November 11 and replaced with cold lunches before school leadership intervened, Richfield Public Schools told CNN last week.After a video surfaced on social media of a cafeteria worker throwing away lunches, the community responded with anger.The district apologized and asked people to donate to help pay off the lunch debt -- and people opened their pocketbooks in droves.People have donated more than $22,800 since the incident, the district told CNN on Tuesday. That exceeds the outstanding lunch account balance of $19,669.Read More
