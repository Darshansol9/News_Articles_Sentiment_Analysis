(CNN) Honda is recalling 1.6 million vehicles in the last phase of its efforts to replace potentially deadly Takata airbags, the company announced Friday.The recall affects Honda and Acura automobiles in the United States.An airbag of a Honda vehicle in TokyoHonda urged owners of affected vehicles to seek free replacements immediately at authorized dealers.Honda is ahead of the National Highway Traffic Safety Administration's schedule for replacing the Takata airbag inflators, the carmaker said in a statement. Honda said it has made "significant progress with existing recall repairs," with an 83 percent completion rate. The company has enough replacement parts, all from alternative suppliers, to repair affected Acura and Honda models, it said.The airbag issue led Takata to file for bankruptcy in June 2017.Read More
