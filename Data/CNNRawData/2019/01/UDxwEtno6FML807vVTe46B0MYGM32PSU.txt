(CNN) Ben & Jerry's is the latest company announcing plans to reduce its environmental footprint.The ice cream maker says it'll stop offering plastic straws and spoons in more than 600 stores worldwide. It estimates the change could eliminate 30 million spoons and 2.5 million straws annually.The company expects to fully transition to paper straws and wooden spoons by April 9, this year's date for its popular Free Cone Day. The straws will be available by request only.By the end of 2020, Ben & Jerry's hopes to shift away from clear plastic cups, plastic-lined cups and plastic lids as well.Of course, the easiest way to avoid plastic still is to get your ice cream in a waffle cone.
